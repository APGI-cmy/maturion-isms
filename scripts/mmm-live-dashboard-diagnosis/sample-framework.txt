Information Security Management Framework

Domain 1: Governance and Risk Management

1.1 Policy and Standards
  Criteria 1.1.1: Organisation has documented information security policies aligned to business objectives.
  Criteria 1.1.2: Security policies are reviewed and approved by senior management at least annually.
  Criteria 1.1.3: Roles and responsibilities for information security are clearly defined.

1.2 Risk Assessment
  Criteria 1.2.1: A formal risk register is maintained and reviewed quarterly.
  Criteria 1.2.2: Risk treatment plans are documented, assigned, and tracked to closure.
  Criteria 1.2.3: Risk appetite and tolerance thresholds are defined and communicated.

Domain 2: Access Control

2.1 User Authentication
  Criteria 2.1.1: Multi-factor authentication is enforced for all privileged and remote access.
  Criteria 2.1.2: Password policies meet minimum complexity and rotation requirements.
  Criteria 2.1.3: Privileged access is reviewed and recertified at least quarterly.

2.2 Identity Management
  Criteria 2.2.1: User provisioning and de-provisioning processes are documented and followed.
  Criteria 2.2.2: Access rights are reviewed annually and upon role change.
  Criteria 2.2.3: Dormant accounts are disabled within 90 days of inactivity.

Domain 3: Incident Management

3.1 Incident Detection
  Criteria 3.1.1: Security monitoring tools are deployed across critical assets.
  Criteria 3.1.2: Alerting thresholds are defined for key security events.
  Criteria 3.1.3: A 24x7 monitoring capability is in place for critical systems.

3.2 Incident Response
  Criteria 3.2.1: An incident response plan is documented, tested, and maintained.
  Criteria 3.2.2: Incident classification criteria (P1-P4) are defined and communicated.
  Criteria 3.2.3: Post-incident reviews are conducted within 5 business days for P1/P2 events.
