# Process Integrity

# MPS 6 – Quality Assurance and Process Integrity

## Intent
Ensure that the orebody is understood, and that the processing system and controls are designed, implemented and maintained in such a way that maximises diamond liberation and recovery, detects anomalies, and minimises opportunities for loss or diversion of product.

## Criteria

**6.1**  
Operations will maintain a clear understanding of the orebody characteristics including diamond size frequency distributions and variability across mining areas.

**6.2**  
Sampling and analysis processes will be implemented to confirm diamond size frequency distribution and characteristics.

**6.3**  
Granulometry analysis will be performed regularly to monitor ore characteristics and processing performance.

**6.4**  
Diamond liberation performance will be monitored and reviewed monthly.

**6.5**  
Process modelling and simulation will be used where appropriate to optimise diamond recovery performance.

**6.6**  
Blasting practices will be designed and controlled to optimise liberation while preventing unnecessary diamond breakage.

**6.7**  
Monitoring systems will be implemented to assess the effectiveness of the recovery process.

**6.8**  
Recovery efficiency will be monitored through production reconciliation.

**6.9**  
Process audits will be conducted periodically to ensure recovery systems are operating effectively.

**6.10**  
Stockpile management processes will ensure accurate accounting for ore and diamond-bearing material.

**6.11**  
Waste removal processes will ensure that potential diamond-bearing material is not incorrectly discarded.

**6.12**  
Production reconciliation processes will be implemented to compare recovered diamonds against expected recovery.

**6.13**  
Equipment specifications and performance requirements will be documented and maintained.

**6.14**  
Tailings audits will be conducted to ensure diamonds are not being lost through the process.

**6.15**  
External audits of the process may be conducted periodically to validate recovery performance.

# MPS 7 – Process Control and Operational Failure Management

## Intent
Ensure that operational processes remain controlled, measurable and auditable, and that failures or abnormal conditions are properly detected, recorded and investigated.

## Criteria

**7.1**  
Mining and security teams will coordinate operational activities where diamonds may be exposed.

**7.2**  
Control-loop configurations and process automation settings will be documented.

**7.3**  
Systems will be implemented to monitor manipulation risks in recovery processes.

**7.4**  
Operational process data will be captured and retained for audit purposes.

**7.5**  
Abnormal operational conditions will be recorded and investigated.

**7.6**  
Operational failures will trigger defined response procedures.

**7.7**  
Maintenance activities affecting recovery systems will be documented and authorised.

**7.8**  
Access to process control systems will be restricted and monitored.

**7.9**  
Operators will be trained to detect abnormal conditions that may affect diamond recovery.

**7.10**  
Personnel behaviour in diamond areas will follow defined security protocols.

**7.11**  
Personnel will avoid hand-to-mouth movements while working in diamond handling areas.

**7.12**  
Personnel will present clean hands to cameras when required by security procedures.

**7.13**  
Personnel will minimise product handling wherever possible.

**7.14**  
Items entering diamond areas will be strictly controlled.

**7.15**  
Photography and recording devices will be prohibited unless authorised.

**7.16**  
Suspicious behaviour or movements will be investigated.

---

# MPS 8 – Maintenance and Housekeeping

## Intent
Ensure that facilities and equipment are maintained in a condition that supports secure diamond processing and minimises opportunities for product loss.

## Criteria

**8.1**  
Maintenance activities affecting diamond recovery circuits will be planned and authorised.

**8.2**  
Shutdown inspections of recovery circuits will be conducted.

**8.3**  
Dual accountability will apply to inspections of recovery equipment.

**8.4**  
Equipment will be classified according to risk and maintenance requirements.

**8.5**  
Preventative maintenance programmes will be implemented for critical equipment.

**8.6**  
Maintenance work in diamond areas will be supervised and documented.

**8.7**  
Spillages will be reported and cleared according to defined procedures.

**8.8**  
Waste removal processes will ensure material is not incorrectly discarded.

**8.9**  
Waste will be removed within defined timeframes.

**8.10**  
Storage areas will be clearly demarcated.

**8.11**  
Housekeeping standards will be maintained in diamond processing areas.

**8.12**  
Equipment causing spillages will be repaired promptly.

**8.13**  
Regular housekeeping inspections will be conducted.

**8.14**  
Security and operational teams will jointly monitor housekeeping standards.

---

# MPS 9 – Management of Change

## Intent
Ensure that risks associated with operational, technical or procedural changes are properly identified, assessed and controlled.

## Criteria

**9.1**  
Formal change management procedures will be implemented.

**9.2**  
Changes to facilities, equipment, processes or procedures will require management approval.

**9.3**  
Security risk assessments will be conducted before significant changes are implemented.

**9.4**  
Changes will be documented and recorded.

**9.5**  
Personnel will be trained on applicable change management requirements.

**9.6**  
Operational documentation will be updated following approved changes.

**9.7**  
Post-change reviews will be conducted to confirm the effectiveness of implemented changes.

**9.8**  
Security controls will be reviewed following operational changes.

**9.9**  
The Security Control Committee may review significant operational changes affecting diamond security.

# MPS 10 – Sales Controls, Ethics, and Fraud Prevention

## Intent
To protect the integrity of diamond sales batches, preserve diamond value, eliminate diamond loss, ensure the longevity for the demand for diamonds and customer retention.

## Criteria

### Access control

**10.1**  
All diamond processing operations should be conducted inside an impervious shell and/or a security-controlled perimeter that minimises the risk of unauthorised access to diamonds and/or diamondiferous material.

**10.2**  
Early warning detection systems shall report unauthorised access attempts. Such attempts shall be investigated immediately, and all false alarms eliminated to not create repetitive false alarms.

**10.3**  
All diamond handling areas and associated process units will be protected by means of physical barriers that are implemented pre-emptively through the identified risk profile to prevent unauthorised access.

**10.4**  
Security systems design will be integrated and automated, supporting production where technology used does not compromise security.

**10.5**  
Access control data will be submitted to predictive trend and pattern analysis conducted to determine risk profiles with integrated intelligent systems that automatically flag and drive the risk offensive.

**10.6**  
For each occurrence where diamonds are accessed or processed whether it be diamond batches, diamond parcels, diamond shipments and/or diamond consignments there should be a clear audit trail recording the persons involved, the time duration of involvement as well as the nature of activities that were performed.

---

### Procedures

**10.7**  
All procedures will be:

- Current, signed off and comply with the internal requirements and templates for procedural design.  
- Readily available for all relevant parties and stakeholders to access.  
- Communicated to all relevant parties and stakeholders.  
- Implemented through the establishment of a monitoring process to verify the implementation thereof.  
- Reviewed continually.

---

### Containment of material

**10.8**  
There will be procedure(s) in place that govern the opening and removal of diamonds from technical sorting equipment following a blockage, stoppage, or anomaly.

**10.9**  
Diamond movement between buildings and facilities should be performed securely by following the requirements stipulated under MPS 19 and controls should be in place to avoid any diamond movement from taking place without security being involved.

---

### Exposure to diamonds

**10.10**  
There will be procedure(s) in place that govern reporting and rectification of all diamond drops, pick-ups and spillages within the diamond processes.

This procedure should also specify the requirements that should be met when detaining people pending the resolving of weight reconciliation discrepancies to ensure that the human rights of people related to privacy and freedom of movement is not interfered with.

---

### Process auditability

**10.11**  
System information contained in the Stock Management System will be provided in a clear unambiguous format while Stock Management System reports are to be collated with other security related information.

**10.12**  
Sales operations will:

- Establish reconciliation points within the production pipeline.  
- Report all notifications of anomalies via the stock management system as well as the Security Incident Management system to management and security staff.  
- Conduct regular (targeted and random) audits to confirm that physical diamonds are recorded and accounted for by the stock management system.  
- Provide information for enhanced audit investigations based on reports received from the Stock Management System to analyse information to determine trends.

---

### Weighing and reconciliation

**10.13**  
There will be procedure(s) in place that govern the way in which weight reconciliation discrepancies or out of tolerances (OTL’s) should be handled and escalated.

**10.14**  
All weighing discrepancies that are out of tolerance (OTL) should be escalated in accordance with procedure and recorded by reporting it in the security incident management system.

**10.15**  
An OTL report should be generated to be analysed by an appropriate qualified person or entity independent from the diamond processing operations at regular intervals as agreed.  
The results should be communicated to a designated audience including but not limited to the Risk Manager: Security, who should include this report as part of the overall security metrics.

**10.16**  
An investigation into OTL’s should be properly recorded with all evidence captured for proper management oversight and to enable remote assurance.

**10.17**  
The principle of “two-person accountability” should always be applied during consignment and/or batch weighing and reconciliation to avoid human error.

**10.18**  
Diamond scales will be calibrated by BOBS (The Botswana Bureau of Standards) at least twice bi-annually and evidence kept for auditing purposes.

**10.19**  
BOBS calibrated scales will be counter-checked daily by means of check weighing.

**10.20**  
Each time a diamond weighing discrepancy is detected, check weighing should be performed to verify scale integrity.

---

### Batch control

**10.21**  
Diamond batches will always be accompanied by system generated parcelling letters against which reconciliation will take place.

**10.22**  
Individual diamonds as well as diamond batches will be packed, parcelled and transported in approved containers only.

**10.23**  
Apart from the parcelling letters, container contents will be identified by means of barcoded labels.

**10.24**  
At each reconciliation point within the system container contents will be verified by:

- Scanning barcodes on containers with scanners directly connected to the stock management system.  
- Performing positive identification through weight reconciliation.

**10.25**  
Manual capturing of barcode labels is prohibited and should only be overridden through a formal change management process.

**10.26**  
Performing unauthorised weight reconciliation off-line is prohibited and should be recorded as a security incident.

**10.27**  
Off-line weight reconciliation may only be authorised through the change management process in the event of system failures and additional security controls must be assigned.

**10.28**  
Diamond weights should not be captured manually. Scales should be linked directly to the stock management system to enable automated recording.

**10.29**  
Permission to proceed with processing after reconciliation discrepancies must follow a two-person accountability process including security and stock control.

**10.30**  
All authorised procedural breaches must be recorded in the security incident management system with proof of authorisation.

---

### System controls

**10.31**  
Import weight reconciliation reports to government will be system generated with automated alerts.

**10.32**  
Diamond reconciliation system access will be controlled through biometric identification.

**10.33**  
Access to the reconciliation system will be automatically removed when no longer required.

**10.34**  
Diamond weight reconciliation systems will prevent further processing unless reconciliation falls within tolerance and criteria are complied with.

---

### Handling non-diamonds

**10.35**  
Non-diamonds or gangue found in diamond batches will be:

- Checked to verify non-diamond status using approved technology.  
- Reconciled against batch weight.  
- Removed in a controlled manner.  
- Handled as if they were diamonds.  
- Sealed in containers.  
- Dispatched back to the mine.  
- Verified again and discarded in a controlled manner.

**10.36**  
The entire process will be documented in a procedure and recorded with an audit trail.

---

### Batch accountability

**10.37**  
At the end of each day a system generated batch check with automated alerts will confirm that all batches have been returned to overnight storage.

---

### Diamond damage prevention

**10.38**  
Diamond processing desks will be designed to be drop friendly.

**10.39**  
Desks will have raised edges to prevent diamonds from falling.

**10.40**  
Floors in diamond handling areas will be carpeted.

**10.41**  
Desks will have partitioning to prevent batch contamination between sorting stations.

---

### Power supply and redundancy

**10.42**  
Diamond processing and security systems will have backup power supply systems.

**10.43**  
Backup systems will ensure uninterrupted operational functionality during grid failures.

---

### Systems failure

**10.44**  
There will be a standing operating procedure for systems failure.

**10.44.1**  
Systems failures must be immediately reported and recorded.

**10.44.2**  
Diamonds must immediately be separated from human contact.

**10.44.3**  
Weight reconciliation must be performed immediately after system restoration.

---

### Key management

**10.45**  
Procedures will govern the management of safe and vault keys.

**10.46**  
All keys will be managed through electronic key management systems linked to access management.

**10.47**  
Key management data will be analysed and included in security metrics.

**10.48**  
Breaches of key management procedures must be reported and investigated.

---

### Additional criteria

**10.49**  
Minimum requirements for diamond shipment, reconciliation, stock control and sales must be defined to ensure compliance with insurance and government requirements.

**10.50**  
Due diligence will be conducted at agreed intervals to verify compliance with insurance requirements and government expectations.

---

# MPS 11 – Trading Operations

## Intent
To provide additional audit criteria unique to trading operations over and above those stipulated under MPS 10 which is also relevant for activities and operations stipulated under MPS 11.

## Criteria

**11.1**  
Trading operations will comply with all relevant criteria under MPS 10 in addition to the specific controls outlined under MPS 11.

**11.2**  
Access control and perimeter security will be implemented within diamond trading facilities.

**11.3**  
All trading areas will maintain a full audit trail of diamond handling activities.

**11.4**  
Secure storage facilities will be implemented for diamonds awaiting trading or shipment.

**11.5**  
Two-person accountability will be applied to diamond handling activities within trading operations.

**11.6**  
Stock management systems will record all diamond movements within trading operations.

**11.7**  
Automated reconciliation processes will be used where possible to reduce human intervention.

**11.8**  
Diamond shipments will follow secure transport procedures.

**11.9**  
Sensitive communications relating to trading operations will be protected through encryption and access controls.
