# Process Integrity

# MPS 6 – Quality Assurance and Process Integrity

## Intent
Ensure that the orebody is understood, and that the processing system and controls are designed, implemented and maintained in such a way that maximises diamond liberation and recovery, detects anomalies, and minimises opportunities for loss or diversion of product.

## Criteria

**6.1**  
Operations will maintain a clear understanding of the orebody characteristics including diamond size frequency distributions and variability across mining areas.

**6.2**  
Sampling and analysis processes will be implemented to confirm diamond size frequency distribution and characteristics.

**6.3**  
Granulometry analysis will be performed regularly to monitor ore characteristics and processing performance.

**6.4**  
Diamond liberation performance will be monitored and reviewed monthly.

**6.5**  
Process modelling and simulation will be used where appropriate to optimise diamond recovery performance.

**6.6**  
Blasting practices will be designed and controlled to optimise liberation while preventing unnecessary diamond breakage.

**6.7**  
Monitoring systems will be implemented to assess the effectiveness of the recovery process.

**6.8**  
Recovery efficiency will be monitored through production reconciliation.

**6.9**  
Process audits will be conducted periodically to ensure recovery systems are operating effectively.

**6.10**  
Stockpile management processes will ensure accurate accounting for ore and diamond-bearing material.

**6.11**  
Waste removal processes will ensure that potential diamond-bearing material is not incorrectly discarded.

**6.12**  
Production reconciliation processes will be implemented to compare recovered diamonds against expected recovery.

**6.13**  
Equipment specifications and performance requirements will be documented and maintained.

**6.14**  
Tailings audits will be conducted to ensure diamonds are not being lost through the process.

**6.15**  
External audits of the process may be conducted periodically to validate recovery performance.

# MPS 7 – Process Control and Operational Failure Management

## Intent
Ensure that operational processes remain controlled, measurable and auditable, and that failures or abnormal conditions are properly detected, recorded and investigated.

## Criteria

**7.1**  
Mining and security teams will coordinate operational activities where diamonds may be exposed.

**7.2**  
Control-loop configurations and process automation settings will be documented.

**7.3**  
Systems will be implemented to monitor manipulation risks in recovery processes.

**7.4**  
Operational process data will be captured and retained for audit purposes.

**7.5**  
Abnormal operational conditions will be recorded and investigated.

**7.6**  
Operational failures will trigger defined response procedures.

**7.7**  
Maintenance activities affecting recovery systems will be documented and authorised.

**7.8**  
Access to process control systems will be restricted and monitored.

**7.9**  
Operators will be trained to detect abnormal conditions that may affect diamond recovery.

**7.10**  
Personnel behaviour in diamond areas will follow defined security protocols.

**7.11**  
Personnel will avoid hand-to-mouth movements while working in diamond handling areas.

**7.12**  
Personnel will present clean hands to cameras when required by security procedures.

**7.13**  
Personnel will minimise product handling wherever possible.

**7.14**  
Items entering diamond areas will be strictly controlled.

**7.15**  
Photography and recording devices will be prohibited unless authorised.

**7.16**  
Suspicious behaviour or movements will be investigated.

# MPS 8 – Maintenance and Housekeeping

## Intent
Ensure that facilities and equipment are maintained in a condition that supports secure diamond processing and minimises opportunities for product loss.

## Criteria

**8.1**  
Maintenance activities affecting diamond recovery circuits will be planned and authorised.

**8.2**  
Shutdown inspections of recovery circuits will be conducted.

**8.3**  
Dual accountability will apply to inspections of recovery equipment.

**8.4**  
Equipment will be classified according to risk and maintenance requirements.

**8.5**  
Preventative maintenance programmes will be implemented for critical equipment.

**8.6**  
Maintenance work in diamond areas will be supervised and documented.

**8.7**  
Spillages will be reported and cleared according to defined procedures.

**8.8**  
Waste removal processes will ensure material is not incorrectly discarded.

**8.9**  
Waste will be removed within defined timeframes.

**8.10**  
Storage areas will be clearly demarcated.

**8.11**  
Housekeeping standards will be maintained in diamond processing areas.

**8.12**  
Equipment causing spillages will be repaired promptly.

**8.13**  
Regular housekeeping inspections will be conducted.

**8.14**  
Security and operational teams will jointly monitor housekeeping standards.

# MPS 9 – Management of Change

## Intent
Ensure that risks associated with operational, technical or procedural changes are properly identified, assessed and controlled.

## Criteria

**9.1**  
Formal change management procedures will be implemented.

**9.2**  
Changes to facilities, equipment, processes or procedures will require management approval.

**9.3**  
Security risk assessments will be conducted before significant changes are implemented.

**9.4**  
Changes will be documented and recorded.

**9.5**  
Personnel will be trained on applicable change management requirements.

**9.6**  
Operational documentation will be updated following approved changes.

**9.7**  
Post-change reviews will be conducted to confirm the effectiveness of implemented changes.

**9.8**  
Security controls will be reviewed following operational changes.

**9.9**  
The Security Control Committee may review significant operational changes affecting diamond security.
