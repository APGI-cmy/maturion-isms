# The Company Diamond Control Standard (LDCS)

---

# Leadership and Governance

## MPS 1 – Leadership

### Intent
To set clear expectations for Security Management that are codified with a policy and supporting procedures which are consistently applied by leaders at all levels.

### Required Actions

#### 1.1 Security Policy
A Security Policy signed by the most senior executive for The Company, applicable to both:

- Karowe Diamond Mine (KDM)
- Corporate Office at the Diamond Technology Park (DTP)

The policy should be prominently displayed and communicated via:

- Email
- Internal platforms (e.g., Yammer)

At KDM it should be displayed at:

- Blue Gate (Personnel Control Centre)
- Entry to the process plant
- Recovery building
- Diamond handling areas

#### 1.2 Policy Content
The policy will be a short document outlining:

- The Company’s obligations regarding security
- Individual obligations regarding security-related activities

#### 1.3 Induction Integration
The policy will be incorporated into induction processes for:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Visitors

Records must confirm understanding and agreement.

#### 1.4 Leadership Application
Leaders must make the security policy relevant within their workplaces by:

- Establishing **Golden Rules** aligned with risk profiles
- Conducting **bi-weekly security awareness sessions**

#### 1.5 Role Accountability
Security responsibilities must be reflected in role descriptions for:

- High-risk diamond areas
- Security teams
- Management

Consultation with HR may be required to enhance role definitions.

#### 1.6 Security Culture
Leadership teams must regularly assess:

- Security culture
- Adherence to protocols
- Compliance during high-risk activities

Examples include:

- Maintenance work
- Shutdowns
- Clearing blockages or spillages

# MPS 2 – Chain of Custody and Security Control Committee

## Intent
To ensure clear accountability for the custody of diamond material from extraction to shipment.

## Required Actions

### 2.1 Chain of Custody Matrix
Each operation must maintain a **Chain of Custody Matrix** identifying:

- Accountable manager
- Security controls under their responsibility

### 2.2 Accountability Scope
Personnel across operations are included, including:

- Managing Director (MD)
- General Manager (GM)
- Mining Operations
- Support Services

### 2.3 Review Cycle
The chain of custody document must be reviewed:

- Annually
- After personnel or process changes

### 2.4 Security Responsibility
Managers responsible for chain-of-custody elements must ensure:

- Security of personnel
- Protection of property
- Safeguarding of product
- Protection of company reputation

These managers form the **Security Control Committee (SCC).**

### 2.5 RACI Framework
Roles must be documented using a **RACI chart**:

- Responsible
- Accountable
- Consulted
- Informed

### 2.6 Security Control Committee
The SCC will be chaired by the **most senior executive** of the operation.

### 2.7 SCC Charter
The committee will operate under an approved charter and will:

- Approve joint operational/security procedures
- Address root causes of spillage or maintenance risks
- Review team dynamics in high-risk areas
- Monitor product quality indicators
- Guide security investigations
- Review security department performance
- Approve facility design changes

### 2.8 Meeting Frequency
The SCC must meet **at least four times per year**.

Meeting records must include:

- Minutes
- Decisions
- Assigned responsibilities

# MPS 3 – Separation of Duties

## Intent
To reduce risks of fraud or error by separating responsibilities.

## Required Actions

### 3.1 Segregation of Duties
Responsibilities related to diamonds should be separated between:

1. Custody
2. Authorisation
3. Record keeping
4. Reconciliation

### 3.2 Security Functional Separation
Security responsibilities should be separated between:

- Security Operations
- Technical Security Systems
- Investigations

### 3.3 Organisational Independence
Security must remain independent from operational diamond processing management.

### 3.4 Operational Boundaries
Security personnel must **not participate in operational diamond processes**.

### 3.5 Security Leadership
The Company must appoint a **Risk Manager: Security** responsible for:

- Karowe Diamond Mine
- Diamond Technology Park

Supported by:

- Security Superintendents
- Operational Support Coordinators

### 3.6 Accountability
The Risk Manager: Security is responsible for delivering security operations in accordance with:

- Laws and regulations
- Corporate policy
- This standard

### 3.7 Reporting Line
The Risk Manager reports directly to the **Chief Risk Officer**.

### 3.8 Executive Interaction
The Risk Manager must interact with the senior executive **at least twice monthly**.

### 3.9 Support to Departments
The Risk Manager supports Heads of Department in implementing security controls.

### 3.10 Metrics
Security performance must be tracked using metrics reported to the SCC.

# MPS 4 – Risk Management

## Intent
To ensure resources focus on the highest risks using a structured risk management framework.

## Required Actions

### 4.1 Risk Framework
The Company has adopted a **Risk Management Policy and Framework.**

### 4.2 Governance Structure
The framework defines responsibilities for:

- Board of Directors
- Audit & Risk Committee
- Managing Director
- Executive Management
- Staff

### 4.3 Supporting Documentation
Security risk management must align with:

- ERM Policy
- ERM Framework

### 4.4 External Standards
Security risk assessments should consider best practice standards including:

- ISO 31000
- NIST Risk Framework
- Mineral Industry Risk Management Framework

### 4.5 Risk Tools
Personnel must use operational risk tools including:

- CURA (ERM)
- Perspective (Security Incident Management)
- Isometrix (SHE Management)

### 4.6 Risk Registers
Risk registers must include:

- Risk Impact Rating
- Likelihood Rating
- Control Effectiveness
- Speed of Onset
- Risk Trend
- Risk Tolerance
- Risk Response

### 4.7 Risk Register Reviews
Risk registers must be reviewed regularly.

### 4.8 Risk Control Objective
Security risks must be reduced **as low as reasonably practicable (ALARP).**

### 4.9 High Risk Reviews
High and very high risks must be reviewed **annually**.

### 4.10 Critical Controls
Critical controls must be identified and resourced for high-risk diamond areas.

# Process Integrity

## MPS 6 – Quality Assurance and Process Integrity

### Intent
Ensure orebody understanding and operational process control to maximise revenue recovery and detect anomalies.

### Key Areas

- Diamond Liberation
- Granulometry
- Process Modelling
- Blasting
- Monitoring and Assessment
- Recovery Efficiency
- Process Audits
- Stockpile Management
- Waste Removal
- Reconciliation and Accounting

### Key Requirements

- Establish **Diamond Size Frequency Distributions (DSFD)**
- Conduct **granulometry analysis**
- Perform **monthly liberation performance reviews**
- Conduct **tailings audits**
- Track **production reconciliation**
- Maintain **equipment specifications databases**
- Implement **process simulations**
- Conduct **annual external process audits**

# MPS 7 – Process Control and Operational Failure Management

## Intent
Ensure operational processes remain controlled, measurable, and auditable.

### Key Requirements

- Coordinate mining and security
- Maintain documented control-loop configurations
- Monitor manipulation risks in recovery systems
- Establish clear procedures for abnormal process conditions
- Maintain documentation for all operational and maintenance activities

### Behavioural Rules in Diamond Areas

Personnel must:

- Avoid hand-to-mouth movements
- Show clean hands to cameras
- Minimise product handling
- Limit items entering diamond areas
- Avoid photography without permission
- Avoid suspicious movements

# MPS 8 – Maintenance and Housekeeping

## Intent
Ensure diamond facilities remain clean, orderly, and subject to planned maintenance.

### Maintenance

- Planned shutdown inspections
- Dual accountability for recovery circuit inspections
- Equipment classification based on risk

### Housekeeping

- Clearly demarcated storage areas
- Waste removal within **12 hours**
- Regular inspections by SCC members
- Immediate repair of spillage-causing equipment
