# Maturion Framework Governance Strategy

## Purpose

This document captures the agreed strategic direction for the Maturion ISMS maturity roadmap engine, framework compiler, governance workflow and operational resilience architecture.

The objective is to preserve architectural intent, align future implementation work and prevent strategic drift during development.

This document also captures the broader platform philosophy underpinning Maturion.

Maturion is not intended to become static compliance software.

It is intended to become:

- a living operational intelligence ecosystem
- a governance orchestration environment
- a resilience maturity operating system
- an evidence-driven organisational nervous system

# Current Confirmed Architecture

## Stage 1 — Framework Generation Wizard

The new wizard is operational and currently supports:

- Upload verbatim
- Create from scratch
- Hybrid generation (future phase)

The current workflow:

1. User uploads framework
2. AI parses framework
3. AI proposes domains
4. User reviews framework
5. User clicks Compile
6. User enters Framework Configuration Workspace

The Upload Verbatim route is the first implementation priority because it allows rapid onboarding of existing standards.

# Core Layered Architecture

The maturity roadmap is structured as a layered governance model.

## Layer 1 — Domains

Primary governance domains:

1. Leadership and Governance
2. Process Integrity
3. People and Culture
4. Protection
5. Proof It Works

Domains:

- act as dashboard containers
- represent maturity aggregation levels
- contain MPS structures
- support approval and governance workflows
- maintain independent maturity states

---

## Layer 2 — Minimum Performance Standards (MPS)

Each domain contains a minimum of five MPS structures.

Examples:

- MPS 1 – Leadership
- MPS 2 – Chain of Custody
- MPS 3 – Separation of Duties

The AI compiler must:

- generate industry-specific MPS structures
- personalise outputs to the organisation
- align to international best practices
- reflect operational context
- incorporate real-time situational awareness

Each MPS contains:

- numbering
- maturity descriptors
- tutorial guidance
- AI chat interface
- edit/recompile workflow
- governance approval chain

---

## Layer 3 — Intent Statements

Each MPS contains an intent statement.

The intent explains:

- WHY the MPS exists
- WHAT operational outcome is required
- WHAT business objective is protected

Intent statements:

- are collapsible
- are editable through AI proposed altering mechanisms
- support full governance approvals
- form part of the audit trail

---

## Layer 4 — Assessment Criteria

Assessment criteria form the core operational control layer.

Criteria:

- are linked to domains and MPS structures
- are sequentially numbered
- support AI generation and refinement
- support evidence mapping
- support maturity scoring
- support approval workflows

Each criteria card contains:

- tutorial guidance
- AI interaction
- maturity indicators
- evidence linkage
- scoring visibility
- compliance summaries

The AI must generate:

- organisation-specific criteria
- industry-aware criteria
- standards-aligned criteria
- operationally aware criteria

---

## Layer 5 — Maturity Descriptors

Each criterion contains maturity descriptors across:

1. Basic
2. Reactive
3. Compliant
4. Proactive
5. Resilient

These descriptors define:

- current operational state
- target operational state
- evidence expectations
- automation maturity
- governance sophistication
- resilience capability

This layer differentiates Maturion from conventional audit software.

The platform does not merely verify compliance.

It measures:

- maturity
- operational capability
- resilience
- automation
- predictive governance
- organisational evolution

---

## Layer 6 — Evidence Management

Evidence management activates after framework publication.

Evidence sources include:

- uploaded documents
- linked operational systems
- PIT integration
- risk management systems
- incident management systems
- external platforms
- future automation integrations

The AI:

- evaluates evidence
- proposes maturity levels
- explains scoring outcomes
- identifies gaps
- recommends next actions

Evidence workflows support:

- approval loops
- escalation

- auditor intervention

# Approval Architecture

## Level 1 — Working Approval

User approves AI-generated content.

Characteristics:

- editable
- audit tracked
- iterative
- non-final

Applies at:

- criteria level
- intent level
- MPS level

---

## Level 2 — Domain Governance Approval

Domain-level approval introduces governance lock-in.

Characteristics:

- dual responsibility
- segregation of duties
- tracked approval conversations
- change recommendations
- controlled unlocking
- governance traceability

Once approved:

- the domain becomes locked
- modifications require formal change management

---

## Level 3 — Executive Sign-Off

Final enterprise approval.

Characteristics:

- executive governance
- full framework approval
- enterprise lock-in
- policy implementation triggering
- organisational publication

The final framework becomes:

- an official organisational standard
- operational governance baseline
- live maturity roadmap
